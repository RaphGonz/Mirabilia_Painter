<objective>
Implement the DDPG replay buffer (DDPG-04): a pre-allocated numpy ring buffer holding 200k transitions, storing canvas channels as uint8 and the step channel as a float32 scalar, returning fully-formed float32 7-channel observation tensors on sample.

Purpose: The replay buffer is the experience store for off-policy DDPG. Storing 6 canvas channels as uint8 (not float32) and the step value as a scalar (not a tiled 64×64 spatial channel) keeps the 200k-transition buffer at ~9.84 GB instead of ~39 GB — fitting the machine's 31 GB RAM. It is standalone (no model dependency) and runs in parallel with the actor (Plan 03-01) in Wave 1.
Output: `ddpg/replay_buffer.py` (ReplayBuffer class) + `tests/test_replay_buffer.py`, all tests green.

## Phase Goal

**As a** training-loop builder, **I want to** push and sample transitions through a memory-bounded ring buffer, **so that** Phase 4 can train DDPG off-policy without exhausting RAM and receives ready-to-use 7-channel float32 observations.
</objective>
